\section{Background Information}

Barcelona has a population of 1,615,448 in urban area in 2011, with 90,543 foreigners. 
Among all the 61,720 Asian immigrates, Chinese make up for 26%. 
The annual income for people in the urban area is €12,031 per person. \cite{einstein}
Last year, 7,571,766 tourists have visited Barcelona. 
There are more than 5,800 registered restaurants in Barcelona, 80 of which serve Chinese food. 
Only one sells hotpot. \cite{latexcompanion}

\section{Business Ownership}

HH Hotpot has is a restaurant managed by two partners. 
One manages the business inside the restaurant, while the other expands its business outside. 
HH Hotpot is funded by these two partners with their own savings and a 3-year loan from Bank of La Caixa.

\section{Start-up Summary}

HH Hotpot aims to position itself as a foreign restaurant for locals and a homemade one for Chinese customers. 
Serving Sichuan hotpot and famous Chinese desserts like Chinese pizza, spring rolls, dumplings, etc. HH intends to set its food price relatively higher to guarantee the food quality and profits for roughly €25 per person.\cite{knuthwebsite}
HH posits itself as a unique, impressing, high standard restaurant.
HH has already reached an agreement with Sunrise Travel Agency, Rainbow Travel Agency and Eastern Travel Agency from China to provide food for their tourists. 
HH plans to publish advertisement on city maps, distribute brochures to hotels and register on several important websites.
